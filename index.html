Welcome to Smartcoin.net

We aim to provide a thorough understanding of math-based currencies. Math-based currencies like Bitcoin and Ripple have the potential to disrupt finance on a global scale. The transparent and open nature of decentralized currencies will open up possibilities for everyone to build on top of the decentralized networks. Below you will see a list of more or less established math-based currencies.

  • Bitcoin
  • Feathercoin
  • Litecoin
  • Namecoin
  • Ripple (not open source yet)

    • Currency Markets

    With the rise of virtual currencies several online currency exchanges came to life. The world's largest exchange for Bitcoins is Mt.Gox which was established in 2010. Other major markets for multiple smartcoins are BTC-E and Vircurex. Ripple is besides a virtual currency (XRP) also a payment network. The payment network consists of gateways (currency markets) that handle transactions for the Ripple payment network. A Ripple gateway like Bitstamp can exchange between virtual currencies (e.g. XRP, Bitcoin) and traditional currencies (e.g. USD, EUR).
